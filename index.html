    History of the Grand Canyon

  • The Grand Canyon is located in northern Arizona and is believed to have formed 5-6 million years ago!
  • The canyon has been inhabited ever since the last Ice Age.
  • The first Europeans reached the Grand Canyon in the 1540s.
  • After being protected by President Benjamin Harrison, the canyon became a United States National Park in 1919.

    Nature

  • The higher elevations are where you'll find the forests, while the lower elevations are where you'll find the desert basins.
  • It averages 4,000 feet deep and 10 miles wide.
  • The Park includes multiple major ecosystems, with five life zones and four desrt types.
  • It contains more than 1,500 plants, 355 birds, 89 mammalians, 47 reptiles, 9 amphibians, and 17 fish species!

Highlights

If you love nature, then you'll love the Grand Canyon. It's not only one of the seven natural wonders of the world, but it also contains some of the oldest exposed rock on earth. Even if you aren't a scientist or in love with nature, it'll be a really good opportunity to travel and even have a picnic with your family or friends. When you visit the canyon, you will be pleased to see its natural beauty, get great exercise, and experience the amazing weather.
