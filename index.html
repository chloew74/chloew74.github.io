History of the Grand Canyon

The Grand Canyon is located in northern Arizona and is believed to have formed 5-6 million years ago! The canyon has been inhabited ever since the last Ice Age. It's also one of the biggest canyons in the world, being measured at 270 miles long, 18 miles wide, and a mile deep. The first Europeans reached the Grand Canyon in the 1540s. After being protected by President Benjamin Harrison, the canyon became a United States National Park in 1919.

Why you should go visit

If you love nature, then you'll love the Grand Canyon. It's not only one of the seven natural wonders of the world, but it also contains some of the oldest exposed rock on earth. Even if you aren't a scientist or in love with nature, it'll be a really good opportunity to travel and even have a picnic with your family or friends. When you visit the canyon, you will be pleased to see its natural beauty, get great exercise, and experience the amazing weather.
